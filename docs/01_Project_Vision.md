# 1. Vision Statement

To build the world's most personalized AI-powered chess improvement platform that helps players understand **why they lose, what they should improve, and how to reach their next rating milestone** through intelligent analysis, adaptive training, and personalized coaching.

Unlike traditional chess analysis tools that focus on evaluating individual games, Checkmate AI focuses on evaluating the **player**.

---

# 2. Problem Statement

Modern chess platforms provide excellent game analysis using engines such as Stockfish. While these tools accurately identify inaccuracies, mistakes, blunders, and optimal continuations, they often fail to answer the questions that matter most to players:

- Why do I keep making the same mistakes?
- What are my biggest weaknesses?
- Which skills should I prioritize?
- Am I actually improving?
- What should I practice today?
- Which openings suit my playing style?

Players receive engine evaluations, but not personalized guidance.

Current platforms primarily analyze **positions**.

Checkmate AI aims to analyze **players**.

---

# 3. Mission

Empower chess players with personalized insights and structured improvement plans using artificial intelligence.

The platform should function as a personal chess coach that continuously learns from every game the user plays.

---

# 4. Vision

Become the most intelligent chess improvement platform by combining:

- Engine-based analysis
- Pattern recognition
- Machine learning
- Large Language Models
- Personalized training
- Progress analytics

into one seamless experience.

---

# 5. Target Audience

## Primary Audience

Players rated between **800–1800 Elo** who actively want to improve.

These players:

- Play multiple games every week
- Watch educational content
- Solve puzzles
- Study openings
- Want structured improvement

---

## Secondary Audience

- Chess coaches
- School chess clubs
- College chess clubs
- Online chess communities
- Tournament players

---

# 6. Product Philosophy

Every feature should answer one of these questions:

- What is my biggest weakness?
- Why did I lose?
- What should I learn next?
- How much have I improved?
- What should I practice today?

If a feature does not contribute to player improvement, it should not be included.

---

# 7. Core Value Proposition

Instead of simply telling players the best move...

The platform explains:

- Why the move was incorrect
- What concept was misunderstood
- Whether this mistake is recurring
- How to avoid it in future games
- What to practice next

The goal is to transform raw engine evaluations into actionable learning.

---

# 10. Guiding Principles

## Personalization

Every recommendation should be tailored to the individual player.

---

## Simplicity

Complex chess concepts should be explained in simple language.

---

## Transparency

Users should understand *why* recommendations are made.

---

## Continuous Improvement

The platform should evolve as the player improves.

---

## Data-Driven Decisions

Recommendations must be supported by statistical analysis of the user's games.

# 12. Success Metrics

The success of the platform will be measured through:

## User Metrics

- Daily Active Users (DAU)
- Weekly Active Users (WAU)
- Monthly Active Users (MAU)

---

## Engagement Metrics

- Average session duration
- Games analyzed per week
- Training completion rate
- Daily learning streak

---

## Improvement Metrics

- Reduction in blunders
- Improvement in average accuracy
- Reduction in recurring mistakes
- Growth in tactical rating
- Growth in positional understanding

---

## Business Metrics (Future)

- Premium subscriptions
- Coach accounts
- Club partnerships
- User retention

---

# 13. Out of Scope (Version 1)

The following features are intentionally excluded from the initial release:

- Playing chess online
- Building a chess engine
- Replacing Chess.com or Lichess
- Multiplayer matchmaking
- Live game broadcasting
- Tournament hosting
- Social networking features

These may be considered in future versions.

---

# 14. Product Identity

Checkmate AI is **not** another chess website.

It is an intelligent chess improvement platform.

Players will continue playing on their preferred chess platforms.

Checkmate AI serves as the personalized coaching layer that transforms game data into meaningful insights, adaptive training plans, and measurable improvement.

---

# 15. Elevator Pitch

> Checkmate AI is an AI-powered chess improvement platform that analyzes a player's complete game history instead of individual games. Using engine analysis, pattern recognition, and personalized coaching, it identifies recurring weaknesses, explains mistakes in plain language, and creates adaptive training plans that help players improve faster than traditional analysis tools.

---

# 16. Future Vision

The ultimate goal is to create an AI coach capable of understanding every player's unique style, strengths, weaknesses, habits, and learning patterns.

One day, the platform should feel less like software and more like having a world-class chess coach available at any time.
